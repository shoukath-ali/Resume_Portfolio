My Story

Chapter 01

Chapter 02

Where It All Started

My journey started during my bachelor's degree, where I began as a Software Engineering intern, working extensively with DBMS concepts, SQL queries, and query optimization. That experience sparked my curiosity about how data systems work under the hood, which gradually pulled me deeper into databases and large-scale data systems.

Chapter 03

First Full-Time Role

In my first full-time role, I worked closely with business stakeholders to solve real operational problems. One of my early projects was a data science–driven platform, where I handled the entire lifecycle — ETL, EDA, machine learning, analytics, and reporting. That project solidified my interest in data science and data engineering, motivating me to pursue my master's degree.

Chapter 04

Graduate Studies & Growth

During my master's, I explored multiple subdomains including analytics, cloud platforms, and big data systems, continuously upskilling despite the challenges. After graduating, I joined an organization with a real-world business problem and proposed an end-to-end data platform solution. The platform I built improved operational efficiency by around 40% and reduced manual whiteboarding efforts by nearly 70%, and it became widely adopted across the organization.

Thoughts & Insights

On Building Data Systems

The best data architecture is one that evolves with your business. Start simple, measure everything, and let real requirements guide complexity. Premature optimization in data systems often leads to unmaintainable pipelines.

On AI Integration

AI/ML should augment human decision-making, not replace it. The most successful implementations I've seen focus on clear problem definition first, then choosing the right tools—not the other way around.
